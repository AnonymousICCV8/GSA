Cross-Instance Gaussian Splatting Registration via Geometry-Aware Feature-Guided Alignment

GSA is the first to tackle category-level registration in 3D Gaussian Splatting, leveraging geometry-aware features and a two-step optimization to enable cross-instance alignment.

Abstract

We present Gaussian Splatting Alignment (GSA), a novel method for aligning two independently-built 3D Gaussian Splatting (3DGS) models using a similarity transformation, even when they correspond to different objects within the same category (e.g., two different cars). In contrast, existing methods can only align 3DGS models of the same object (e.g., the same car) and often require the true scale as inputâ€”whereas we estimate it successfully. Our approach leverages viewpoint-guided spherical features to establish robust correspondences and introduces a two-step optimization framework that aligns models while keeping their 3DGS representations fixed. First, we perform an iterative, feature-guided coarse registration, followed by a fine registration step enforcing multi-view feature consistency. While our first step already achieves state-of-the-art performance, the second step further improves results. We comprehensively evaluate our method against SOTA alternatives. In the standard same-object setting, our approach consistently outperforms prior methods, sometimes by a large margin, even when they are given the correct scale. In the more challenging case of different objects within the same category, our method vastly surpasses existing approaches, establishing the first effective framework for category-level GS3D registration and unlocking new applications. Our code will be released upon acceptance.
